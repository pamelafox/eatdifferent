Hi, I’m Pamela Fox. I’ve always been a little bit chubby, and after being told by my doctor that I’d gotten a bit too chubby (“overweight”), I decided to research obesity and nutrition. I discovered that everything I thought I knew was wrong - for example, fat doesn’t make you fat (excessive carbohydrates do) and exercise doesn’t make you thin (but it can make you stronger). Armed with my new knowledge, I changed my eating habits, lost my extra weight (40 lbs/18 kg), and I feel healthier than I’ve ever been.

I feel like I spent my life completely oblivious to how my body works, and now I want to make sure other people give themselves the opportunity to inform themselves and make the right decisions for their body. This site is just one way I hope to do that.

For the short version, I recommend reading "How to lose weight without doing any exercise" or "Eat like a predator, not like prey".

For the long version, I recommend these books:

  • Why We Get Fat
    Gary Taubes

    This book disputes nutrition myths that have been propagated for the last 50 years, and explains how fat is formed in the body and how one can lose weight accordingly. It's based on the research he did for his much longer book, Good Calories, Bad Calories. If you have the time - read that too!

  • The Primal Blueprint
    Mark Sisson

    A description of Mark's philosophy of living like our ancestors lived 10,000 years ago, focusing on the way they ate, but also on getting good sleep, good play, moving slowly alot, sprinting, and lifting heavy things. A great book about getting back to our roots in all parts of life.

  • Fast Food Nation
    Eric Schlosser

    A highly informative book about the effect of modern industrialized agriculture on our food, our society, and our environment.

  • In Defense of Food
    Michael Pollan

    A great read about Michael's opinion on how we should be eating today, and a look at why we are eating so badly. It will make you want to run out and eat some organic leafy vegetables immediately.

Spread the Word

Want to get people talking about this? Wear one of these shirts that rebut the conventional wisdom about America’s favorite foods.

Corn is not a vegetable - it's a grain with little nutritional value. Unfortunately, it's now the #1 ingredient in American processed food, in the form of high fructose corn syrup (sweeter than sugar!) and corn oil (not a good type of fat). It's fine to enjoy the occasional corn on the cob at a BBQ, but steer away from making it the #1 ingredient in your life. Check the labels!

Most people know soda isn't great for you -- but do they know how bad it is? The average can of soda has 39g of sugar, and those grams of sugar go straight to your bloodstream, causing blood sugar spikes and eventually insulin resistance and chronic diseases like diabetes. Instead of drinking soda with your meals, enjoy a glass of water with a lemon wedge or some unsweetened iced tea.

Flour is ground up grains (like wheat) and is the main ingredient in breads, cakes, and pastries. Whatever nutritional value was in the grains to begin with is lost in the refining process, and besides that, grains aren't so great for your health. Love baking? Never fear - you can use almond flour or coconut flour as more nutritional substitutes.

Bread is everywhere now - in sandwiches, burgers, and if it's not already part of the meal, it's served on the side. Multi-grain bread is touted as healthy and part of a balanced diet, but it's all pretty unhealthy. Bread is a source of excessive carbohydrates as well as filled with anti-nutrients (like gluten and phytates). Our recommendation? Order your meals without the bread.
